star tracker

star trackers on spacecraft are used to determine the attitude relative to the stars. the star positions are measured using photosensors (they have p-n junctions which upon absorbing electromagnetic radiation make electron-hole pairs in the depletion region and generate current) and they are compared to the absolute positions from a star catalog. there are 58 stars used for this task, as they constitute the brightest amongst the 6000 visible to the naked eye under optimal conditions. more complex missions use far more, however. links https://spaceflight.nasa.gov/shuttle/reference/shutref/orbiter/avionics/gnc/startracker.html https://github.com/esa/tetra3 https://github.com/oresat/oresat-star-tracker https://ipnpr.jpl.nasa.gov/progress_report/42-161/161L.pdf
